SingleHop In-house Digital & Print Work

Some of my work as an in-house designer for SingleHop, a small web hosting company in Chicago.

PROJECT OVERVIEW

SingleHop (now INAP) was continually evolving digitally. The redesign work below includes a variety of user experience, web, visual, and print projects. The team usually consisted of myself, executives, the director of marketing, development team, and senior copywriter.

ROLE & TOOLS

UX-UI Design, Graphic Design, Print Design, Photoshop, Illustrator, InDesign

Understanding the Opportunity

We had the opportunity to better understand our users wants and needs based on planned collected market research by conducting customer and stakeholder interviews. Our goal was to redesign the site to be more user-friendly with special attention to the mobile site. We also planned a brand refresh to reflect our found target audience and consistently apply our brand across all deliverables.

Personas and Studies

Using insights from a customer panel and knowledge from within the company most notably the sales team, I created micro-personas based on retaining our current customers to document key traits and needs. Personas were also used to guide messaging and other brand strategies.

Guide to the User Interface

As a small company, we didn't develop a full brand guide. However I created a simple UI guide for use in development and to maintain consistency across our sites.

SingleHop.com Site Design

In the site redesign we focused on: existing customer support, pushing our differentiators such as the customer SLA, using in-house photography as much as possible, and a mobile-first navigation.

SingleHop ThinkIT Blog Guide

The SingleHop blog was our second site. It functioned as it's own entity and generated leads to the main site. I created an extension of the main style guide to encompass more direction specific to the blog site.

SingleHop ThinkIT Blog Design

The design was kept very simple and tied to our brand colors. The imagery selection was stock photography so we knew we could use color as the rule of thumb to maintain a cohesive feel.

SingleHop Print Style

The visual branding was applied to a modernized print style as well. Below is an ebook I created with our senior copywriter that was promoted through the new blog. It generated fourteen quality leads in it's first week in rotation (a larger amount for our company size). View the full ebook
