Learning and quoting for life insurance online

How might we explain life insurance 100% online in a way our users understand and engage with?

PROJECT OVERVIEW

This was a two month-long project as in-house Lead UX Designer for TruStage Insurance. The team consisted of myself, the analytics team, and collaboration with other UX designers. The visual design and branding of this work was done primarily from an external agency.

ROLE & TOOLS

Lead UX Design, Content Research, User Testing, Procreate, Sketch, Invision, Usertesting.com

Understanding the Opportunity

We found roughly a third of users interested in life insurance applied for a product they didn't qualify for due to their health. We had an opportunity to better assist these users earlier in the experience by not just focusing on health but also product information. The goal was to enable the user in obtaining their best fit product.

01. UX enhancement: Developing more product content

I performed user studies for a baseline read on what users are seeking but not finding in the current life quoting experience. It opened a flood of further testing opportunities, testing various new content. View the full deck

The most sought-after content by our persona was bite-size, rich, straight-forward content. We translated this into a side by side comparison and FAQs tailored to the users point in the journey that we could use for further testing.

02. UX enhancement: Developing a questionnaire

Many of our competitors use a long Q&A process to guide a high funnel user into the right product. We joined this trend, but used a bite-size approach to stay consistent with our user experience. We designed a flow with four to five questions and a set of custom content experiences as the users end result.

After mapping out the flow and testing the content seen above, we wireframed options for a landing page that would hold the questionnaire and new product content.

We put high fidelity mockups from Sketch to Invision for rounds of user-testing. Below is a summary of user testing the full flow to study users with our top three health conditions.

Below are the high fidelity mockups for testing on the live site. In live testing we strive to learn more about our audience, continue to iterate, and tie user studies in with A/B testing.

The results a user generates via the questionnaire are varied. We designed five different customized interfaces depending on how the questionnaire is answered. The bottom right is one example.

The questionnaire isn't developed yet, but the testing used for the quoting interface and content research has increase our engagement and sales by about 14%.
